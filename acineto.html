Carbapenem Resistance in Acinetobacter baumannii

H. Tutan, B. Mazlumoğlu, L. Genç, E.S. Tanriverdi, Z. Çizmeci, M.E. Bulut, E. Aktaş

From Three Tertiary Centers in Türkiye

Background

Carbapenem resistance via carbapenemase genes like OXA-23, OXA-40, NDM is a serious global threat. CRAb is listed as critical by WHO. Our study analyzes these gene distributions in Türkiye.

Methods

336 CRAb isolates collected across 3 hospitals: Şişli (198), Bakırköy (86), Malatya (52). Resistance genes analyzed using Bioeksen qPCR kits.

Results

  • OXA-51: 336
  • OXA-23: 305 (90.8%)
  • OXA-40: 88 (26.2%)
  • NDM: 21 (6.3%)
  • VIM: 1

Conclusion

Frequent detection of OXA-23, increasing NDM and co-productions indicate complex resistance mechanisms in Türkiye. Local practices shape resistance spread and global infection strategies.
