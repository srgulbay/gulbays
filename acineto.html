Carbapenem Resistance in Acinetobacter baumannii

H. Tutan, B. Mazlumoğlu, L. Genç, E.S. Tanriverdi, Z. Çizmeci, M.E. Bulut, E. Aktaş
Şişli Etfal, Malatya, Bakırköy Hastaneleri

Background

Carbapenem resistance in Gram-negative bacteria via carbapenemase production is a major healthcare concern. CRAb has been classified as a critical priority pathogen by WHO. Genes such as blaOXA-23, blaOXA-40, blaOXA-58, and blaNDM contribute to this resistance. Our study investigates the gene distribution in Türkiye.

Methods

A total of 336 CRAb isolates (198 from Şişli, 86 from Bakırköy, 52 from Malatya) collected between Jan 2023 and Oct 2024 were analyzed. Resistance genes were identified using qPCR kits.

Results
  • blaOXA-51 detected in all isolates
  • blaOXA-23: 305 isolates (90.8%)
  • blaOXA-40: 88 isolates (26.2%)
  • blaNDM: 21 isolates (6.3%)
  • blaVIM: 1 isolate
  • Co-productions: 73 isolates had dual genes; some had triple combinations.
  • Not detected: blaOXA-48, blaOXA-58, blaIMP, blaKPC
Conclusion

blaOXA-23 is the most prevalent carbapenemase gene. Co-production and presence of blaNDM highlight complex resistance. Local practices impact gene distribution. These findings are critical for global resistance monitoring.
