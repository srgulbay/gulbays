Carbapenem Resistance in Acinetobacter baumannii

Türkiye'den 3 Merkezi Kapsayan Moleküler Dağılım Sunumu

H. Tutan, B. Mazlumoğlu, L. Genç, E.S. Tanriverdi, Z. Çizmeci, M.E. Bulut, E. Aktaş

Background

Carbapenem resistance is a major global threat. CRAb is listed as a critical priority pathogen by WHO. Key genes involved are blaOXA-23, blaOXA-40, and blaNDM.

Methods

  • 336 CRAb isolates (Jan 2023 - Oct 2024)
  • 3 Centers: Şişli (198), Bakırköy (86), Malatya (52)
  • Gene detection via Bioeksen qPCR kits

Results

Conclusion

High frequency of OXA-23, notable co-productions with NDM. Resistance mechanisms are complex and locally shaped. Crucial for global infection control policies.
